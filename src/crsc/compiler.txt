3. Strategic Recommendations for SNN Optimization
SNNs benefit significantly from custom kernels due to their high temporal sparsity. To maximize acceleration:
Operator Fusion: Use your custom kernel to fuse the membrane potential update, thresholding, and spike generation into a single GPU pass. This avoids the "memory wall" of reading/writing to global memory for every small operation.
Shared Memory: Since SNNs often require state-tracking (membrane potentials), utilize Shared Memory to keep these states close to the CUDA threads, reducing latency compared to Global Memory.
Sparse Representation: If your SNN is highly sparse, design the kernel to only process active spikes using atomic operations or prefix sums, rather than dense tensor multiplication.
