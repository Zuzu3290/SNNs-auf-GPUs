That means the compiler is responsible for:

identifying spiking operations from PyTorch/snntorch/SpikingJelly code
converting them into a simpler execution graph
deciding what runs on CPU and what runs on GPU
choosing the correct CUDA/PTX kernel
launching through your native layer

It becomes the brain of the runtime.

Its job is to:

observe or receive SNN computations from the PyTorch side
lower them into a simpler internal representation
decide which parts should execute on CPU and which on GPU
schedule execution across timesteps and kernels
launch CUDA code efficiently through a native runtime
remain extensible enough for future optimization research like fusion, PTX generation, and adaptive scheduling

It is a specialized compiler/runtime for SNN-oriented accelerated execution.

Design flow :
ir.hpp
Defines your internal operation format.
This is the language your compiler understands.

lowering.cpp
Takes PyTorch/SNN-side operations and converts them into your IR., Converts PyTorch/FX nodes into your IR nodes.

scheduler.cpp
Decides:
The heart 
CPU or GPU
launch order
maybe whether to fuse simple ops

runtime.cpp
Executes the scheduled plan and talks to backend code.

backend_cuda.cu
Contains the actual CUDA kernel launches and GPU-specific execution.

pybind_module.cpp
Connects C++ compiler code to Python/PyTorch.
